RESEARCHERS

Electroencephalogram (EEG)

Electroencephalogram (EEG) is one method of collecting a measure brain activity. By placing the Electrical Geodesics Inc. 128-channel sensor nets over the head, we can passively record and identify activity in multiple locations of the brain. For our EEG research, participants have a sensor net placed on their head and are asked to play a variety of games on a computer screen. And don’t worry – nothing hurts! Our EGI sensor nets are pain-free!

Video Interactions

To get a better sense of the underpinnings of social interactions, our lab can record videotaped interactions of romantic couples, families, or friends. Participating groups are prompted to have a private, casual conversation with each other while the camera is rolling.

Hormonal Assessments

Saliva samples can relay a variety of information about active hormones in the body during the course of different tasks. Our lab takes periodic assessments of saliva to track any changes in hormone levels and identify physical symptoms of stress and other components.

Daily Assessments

We don’t let the moments pass us by! We send out daily reminders prompting participants to complete a 5-minute survey that helps us identify the changes over the development of youth and early adults. Everyday counts because every daily assessment can earn you points that are redeemable for cash!
